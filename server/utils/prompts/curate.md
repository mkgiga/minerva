# The Problem

AI-generated fiction often contains many issues that detract from the reading experience. Common problems include:

- Excessive verbosity and unnecessary filler content. Examples include long-winded descriptions that do not advance the plot or describe the current events in a concise manner.
- Overly elaborate language that detracts from clarity and readability.
- Repetitive phrases, wording and ideas. The AI may repeat the same information multiple times
- "not with x, but with y" constructions that add unnecessary complexity (delete these and simplify the sentence!!)

## Examples and Explanations

- Excessive Verbosity and Unnecessary Filler Content

    Bad Example:
    
    ```xml
    <narrate>You walk into the main room. The scene that greets you is a miracle of simple, profound domesticity. Your father is sitting at the small wooden table, mending a fishing net, a quiet, contented smile on his face that makes him look ten years younger. And your mother... your mother is standing at the hearth, a simple apron tied around her waist, flipping a piece of bread in a hot pan. She is not frail. She is not pale. She is whole.</narrate>
    ```
    
    Good Example:
    
    ```xml
    <narrate>You walk into the main room. Your father is sitting at the small wooden table, mending a fishing net. Your mother is standing at the hearth, flipping a piece of bread in a hot pan. Her frail disposition is gone—she appears healthy and strong.</narrate>
    ```

    Conclusion:

    The bad example includes excessive details about the characters' appearances and emotions instead of focusing on the actual scene in front of the player. The good example is concise and to the point, describing only what is necessary to understand the situation, all while maintaining the narrative flow on top of retaining the original meaning. Areas where this is most prevalent include expository passages, ambient descriptions, and often times in the very last paragraph of a scene. (These are not hard rules - only my personal observations which may or may not be applicable in every case - rely on your own judgement.)

- Repetitive phrases, wording and ideas. The AI may repeat the same information multiple times, leading to redundancy.

    Bad Example:

    Conclusion:
    Aside from being significantly longer, the bad example repeats the same words such as "profound/profoundly," "simple" and "quiet" several times throughout the passage. The good example eliminates these repetitions and condenses the flow of events into a more respectable length. Naturally, the original meaning is preserved too.
